Section 04

Programme Overview

A brief overview of what I'm applying to — so you have the full picture when crafting your letter.

MSc in Nanotechnology for Medicine and Health Care

University of Oxford  ·  Dept. of Materials / Institute of Biomedical Engineering
October 2026 entry  ·  1 year full-time

This programme sits at the intersection of nanoscience, materials engineering, and clinical medicine. It trains students to design and evaluate nanoscale tools and systems for drug delivery, diagnostics, and therapeutic applications — integrating fundamental physics and chemistry with translational biomedical research.

Core modules include nanomaterials characterisation, drug delivery systems, nanomedicine in cancer and cardiovascular disease, regulatory and ethical frameworks, and a substantial independent research project conducted with Oxford faculty.

My application is anchored around computational nanomedicine research — including work with PhysiCell, nanoHUB, and the pc4nanobio platform — and a research proposal on patient-to-patient protein corona variation as a determinant of PEGylated liposome behaviour in a breast cancer model.

The programme is highly competitive. Admission decisions are made holistically, with particular weight given to research potential, scientific rigour, and clarity of purpose.
