About

Who's behind NanoScope?

I'm Sabah — a biological sciences graduate, former radiologic technologist, and incoming MSc student in Nanotechnology for Medicine and Health Care at the University of Oxford.

NanoScope started as a way to keep up with a field I was teaching myself in the gaps between clinical shifts and coursework. It turned into something more sustained: 15+ articles covering lipid nanoparticles, mRNA vaccine delivery, graphene biosensors, ctDNA liquid biopsy, nanopore sequencing, and oncology drug delivery — all written to be precise without being impenetrable.

My interest is specifically in translation: why systems that work elegantly in controlled settings so often disappoint in real patients, and what it would take to close that gap. If that question interests you too, you're in the right place.
